Semiconductor technology

Semiconductor technology is the field of science and engineering that uses semiconductor materials (mainly silicon) to build electronic components like transistors, diodes, and integrated circuits (chips) that power almost all modern electronics.

In very simple terms

A semiconductor is a material that can act:

  • Sometimes like a conductor (lets electricity flow)
  • Sometimes like an insulator (blocks electricity)

The key idea:

👉 We can control how electricity flows through it

That controllability is what makes computers, phones, and chips possible.

Why it’s called “semi”

  • Conductors (copper): electricity flows freely
  • Insulators (rubber): electricity does not flow
  • Semiconductors (silicon): electricity flows only when we want it to

The core material: Silicon

Silicon is used because:

  • It’s abundant and cheap
  • It’s stable at high temperatures
  • Its electrical behavior can be precisely controlled

Pure silicon doesn’t conduct well.

So we modify it using a process called doping.

Doping (making silicon useful)

Doping means adding tiny amounts of other elements:

N-type silicon

  • Add phosphorus
  • Adds extra electrons
  • Electricity flows via electrons

P-type silicon

  • Add boron
  • Creates “holes” (missing electrons)
  • Electricity flows via holes

By combining P-type and N-type materials, we can control current flow.

Key semiconductor devices

  1. Diode
    • Allows current to flow in one direction only
    • Used in chargers, power supplies
  2. Transistor (most important)
    • Acts as a switch or amplifier
    • Billions exist in modern CPUs
    • Basic roles:
      • ON (1)
      • OFF (0)

This is the foundation of digital logic.

Semiconductor technology in chips

Using advanced manufacturing:

  • Transistors are etched onto silicon wafers
  • Billions fit on a chip smaller than a fingernail
  • Measured in nanometers (e.g., 3 nm, 5 nm)

What semiconductor technology enables

  • CPUs & GPUs
  • Smartphones
  • Memory (RAM, SSDs)
  • AI accelerators
  • Cars, medical devices, satellites

Big picture

Semiconductor technology is:

The art of controlling electrons to process information

Without it:

  • No computers
  • No internet
  • No smartphones
  • No modern AI
