Quantum computing

Quantum computing is a new kind of computing that uses the rules of quantum physics instead of normal electronics.

Simple idea

  • Normal computers use bits:
    • A bit is either 0 or 1
  • Quantum computers use qubits:
    • A qubit can be 0, 1, or both at the same time (this is called superposition)

Very simple example

Normal computer

Imagine a light switch:

  • OFF = 0
  • ON = 1

If you want to test 4 combinations, the computer checks one by one:


00 → 01 → 10 → 11

Quantum computer

Now imagine a magic switch:

  • It can be OFF and ON at the same time

With 2 qubits, a quantum computer can represent:


00, 01, 10, 11

all at once

So instead of testing combinations one by one, it can work on many possibilities simultaneously.

Why this matters

Quantum computers are especially good at:

  • Breaking or analyzing encryption
  • Simulating molecules and chemistry
  • Solving some huge math problems much faster

One-sentence summary

A quantum computer uses qubits that can be 0 and 1 at the same time, allowing it to explore many solutions at once instead of one by one.
