Detail pack in an Inventory Management System

A detail pack in an inventory management system usually refers to a grouped or bundled set of items that are tracked together as one unit, but still maintain their individual details behind the scenes.

Think of it like a “package with breakdown visibility.”

🔹 Simple Example

Imagine you sell a gift box:

Detail Pack: “Holiday Gift Set”

  • 1x Wine bottle
  • 2x Glass cups
  • 1x Chocolate box

In your system:

  • You can sell it as one item (the pack)
  • But internally, the system knows the exact components

🔹 What makes it “detail pack”?

Unlike a simple bundle, a detail pack usually:

  • Keeps item-level details
  • Tracks quantities per component
  • May include cost breakdown per item
  • Allows inventory deduction per component

🔹 How it works in inventory

When you sell 1 “Holiday Gift Set”:

  • Wine stock → -1
  • Glass cups → -2
  • Chocolate → -1

So even though you sell 1 pack, inventory updates happen at the detailed level.

🔹 Why businesses use it

  • Better inventory accuracy
  • Easier cost tracking
  • Useful for:
    • Retail bundles
    • Manufacturing kits
    • Restaurant combos
    • Subscription boxes

🔹 Related terms (you might also see)

  • Bundle / Kit – similar idea, but may not always track details
  • Bill of Materials (BOM) – in manufacturing (more structured version)
  • Composite Product – used in POS systems

🔹 Real-world analogy

A detail pack is like ordering a combo meal:

  • You buy “Combo A”
  • But the system knows it includes:
    • Burger
    • Fries
    • Drink

If you're building something like your TryCastanet platform, a detail pack is powerful for:

  • Subscription bundles
  • Product kits
