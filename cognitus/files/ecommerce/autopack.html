AutoPack

AutoPack in an inventory management system usually refers to a feature that automatically groups or bundles items into predefined packaging units based on rules you set.

Think of it as the system deciding:

“These items should go together in this box/carton/package—no need for manual packing.”

🔧 What AutoPack typically does

Instead of manually assigning items to packages, AutoPack:

  • Automatically creates packages/boxes for orders
  • Assigns items based on:
    • Quantity (e.g., 10 items per box)
    • Weight or volume limits
    • Product type or category
  • Applies predefined packaging rules

📦 Simple Example

Let’s say you sell bottled drinks:

  • Rule: 12 bottles per carton
  • Order: 120 bottles

👉 With AutoPack:

  • System creates 10 cartons automatically
  • Each carton contains 12 bottles

No manual counting or grouping needed.

🧠 Common Use Cases

  • E-commerce fulfillment
  • Warehouse management systems (WMS)
  • Shipping/logistics automation
  • Manufacturing packaging lines

⚙️ Advanced AutoPack Features

In more sophisticated systems, AutoPack can:

  • Optimize packaging to reduce shipping cost
  • Choose the best box size
  • Combine items across SKUs intelligently
  • Integrate with shipping carriers for label generation
  • Handle multi-location warehouses

🚨 Important Note

“AutoPack” is not a universal standard term—its exact behavior depends on the system (e.g., ERP, WMS, or custom platform). Some systems may use it to mean:

  • Auto-bundling
  • Auto-cartonization
  • Auto-packaging logic
