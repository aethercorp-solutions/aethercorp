App Store subscription

In the Apple Inc. ecosystem, the terms “App Store subscription” and “in-app subscription” are often used interchangeably—but technically, there’s a distinction worth understanding (especially since you’re building apps).

🔹 1. In-App Subscription (correct technical term)

This is the actual feature you implement using StoreKit.

What it means:

  • A subscription sold inside your app
  • Managed through Apple’s In-App Purchase (IAP) system
  • Users subscribe without leaving the app

Examples:

  • Monthly premium access in your React Native app
  • SaaS-style features (e.g., AI tools, content unlocks)

Types:

  • Auto-renewable subscriptions (most common)
  • Non-renewing subscriptions (manual renewal)

👉 This is what you implement using:

  • react-native-iap
  • StoreKit APIs
  • App Store Connect subscription products

🔹 2. App Store Subscription (user-facing concept)

This refers to how subscriptions are presented and managed in the App Store ecosystem.

What it means:

  • Subscriptions listed in the App Store
  • Managed via:
    • iPhone Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions
    • App Store UI

Example:

A user sees:

“Your subscriptions: Netflix, Spotify, YourApp”

Even though they subscribed inside the app, Apple surfaces it as an App Store subscription.

🧠 Practical takeaway

If you’re implementing subscriptions in your app:

You are building an In-App Subscription,

which becomes an App Store Subscription from the user’s perspective.

🧠 Simple explanation

The In-App Purchase (IAP) system is the framework provided by Apple Inc. that lets you sell digital products and subscriptions inside your app, with Apple handling payments, security, and user billing.

It’s built using StoreKit.

Think of IAP as:

A built-in payment system for apps

where users can buy things without leaving your app

🔹 What you can sell with IAP

Only digital goods/services, like:

  • Premium features (unlock pro tools)
  • Subscriptions (monthly/yearly plans)
  • Virtual items (coins, credits)
  • Content (ebooks, courses, media)

🚫 Not allowed:

  • Physical goods (use external payments)
  • Real-world services (e.g., ride booking, food delivery)

🔹 How it works (end-to-end flow)

1. You define products

In App Store Connect, you create:

  • Product IDs (e.g., com.trycastanet.premium.monthly)
  • Pricing
  • Subscription duration

2. User initiates purchase

Inside your app:

  • Tap “Subscribe” or “Buy”
  • You call StoreKit (react-native-iap in your case)

3. Apple handles payment

  • Uses Apple ID (Face ID / Touch ID / password)
  • Charges the user
  • Handles taxes, currency, receipts

4. You receive a receipt

  • Apple returns a purchase receipt
  • Your app/backend validates it

5. Unlock content

  • If valid → grant access (premium features, etc.)

1. Consumables

🔹 Types of In-App Purchases

  • Used once, can buy again
  • Example: coins, credits

2. Non-consumables

  • One-time purchase, permanent
  • Example: remove ads, lifetime unlock

3. Subscriptions (most important today)

  • Auto-renewable
  • Example: Netflix-style access

🔹 Why Apple enforces IAP

Apple controls:

  • Payments (security & fraud prevention)
  • User trust
  • Revenue share (commission)

💰 Fees:

  • 30% standard
  • 15% after 1 year (subscriptions)
  • 15% if enrolled in Small Business Program

🔹 Developer architecture (your stack)

Since you’re using:

  • React Native (react-native-iap)
  • AWS Lambda backend

Your real-world flow should be:

  1. App → request purchase
  2. Apple → returns receipt
  3. App → send receipt to backend
  4. Backend → verify with Apple servers
  5. Backend → update MongoDB user subscription
  6. App → unlock features

🔥 Key insight (important)

IAP is not just a payment button —

it’s a full lifecycle system:

  • Purchase
  • Renewal
  • Expiration
  • Refunds
  • Subscription status

⚠️ Common mistake

Many devs think:

“I’ll just trust the app response”

Bad idea.

👉 Always verify receipts on the backend

Otherwise users can fake purchases.

💡 Bottom line

In-App Purchase (IAP) =

Apple’s secure payment + subscription system for digital products inside apps
