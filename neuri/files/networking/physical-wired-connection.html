Physical Wired Connection

A physical (wired) connection refers to the use of physical cables to connect devices for communication or data transfer. These connections are commonly used in networking, audio/video systems, and peripheral device setups.

Common Types of Physical (Wired) Connections

  • Networking (Data Transfer)

    • Ethernet (RJ-45): Most common for local area networks (LANs).
    • Coaxial Cable: Used in cable internet and older TV networks.
    • Fiber Optic Cable: High-speed data transfer using light.

  • Peripheral Devices

    • USB (Universal Serial Bus): Connects keyboards, mice, external storage, etc.
    • HDMI (High-Definition Multimedia Interface): Audio/video connection for displays.
    • DisplayPort / VGA / DVI: Other video connection standards.

  • Power and Charging

    • Power Cords: Deliver electricity to devices.
    • USB-C / Lightning / Micro-USB: Used for charging and data transfer.

  • Audio

    • 3.5mm Audio Jack: Standard for headphones and microphones.
    • RCA Cables: Older audio/video connections for stereo systems and TVs.
    • XLR Cables: Professional audio equipment.

Common Wired Physical Interfaces

Interface Connector Type Purpose Common Uses
USB-A / USB-C Rectangular (USB-A), oval (USB-C) Data transfer, charging, peripheral connection Keyboards, mice, external drives, smartphones
HDMI Flat, wide connector Audio/video transmission Monitors, TVs, projectors, game consoles
DisplayPort Similar to HDMI but with one corner cut High-definition video/audio PC monitors, graphics cards
Ethernet (RJ45) Looks like a large phone plug Wired networking Routers, PCs, network switches
Thunderbolt (USB-C form) USB-C-shaped, supports data/video/power High-speed data, video, charging External GPUs, monitors, fast storage
VGA (older) 15-pin D-sub connector Analog video Legacy monitors and projectors
DVI Rectangular with multiple pins Digital video Older monitors and graphics cards
3.5mm Audio Jack Small round plug Analog audio Headphones, speakers, mics
Power Jack / Barrel Plug Cylindrical plug Power delivery Laptops, routers, external devices
SATA Flat L-shaped connector Internal data transfer Hard drives, SSDs, optical drives
Lightning (Apple) Small proprietary port Data/charging/audio iPhones, iPads (older gens)
