Engine

An engine is a machine that converts energy into mechanical motion to perform work.

In simple terms, it takes some form of energy (like fuel, electricity, or heat) and turns it into movement.

🔧 How an engine works (basic idea)

  1. Energy source – fuel (gasoline, diesel), electricity, steam, etc.
  2. Conversion process – the engine transforms that energy
  3. Output – motion (like turning wheels, spinning a shaft, or moving parts)

🚗 Common types of engines

1. Internal Combustion Engine (ICE)

  • Used in cars, motorcycles, trucks
  • Burns fuel inside the engine
  • Example: gasoline and diesel engines

👉 Fuel burns → creates pressure → moves pistons → rotates crankshaft → powers vehicle

2. Electric Engine (Motor)

  • Used in electric cars, fans, appliances
  • Uses electricity instead of fuel
  • Cleaner and more efficient

👉 Electricity flows → creates magnetic force → spins motor

3. Steam Engine

  • Used in old trains and some industrial machines
  • Uses steam pressure from heated water

⚙️ Where engines are used

  • Vehicles (cars, planes, ships)
  • Machines (factories, generators)
  • Household appliances (fans, washing machines)

🧠 Simple definition

An engine is basically:

“A device that turns energy into motion.”
