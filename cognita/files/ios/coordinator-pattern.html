Coordinator Pattern

The Coordinator Pattern is still robust and relevant in 2025, particularly in iOS development, where it originated and remains most widely used. However, whether it's considered "industry standard" depends on the context:

    • Encourages reusability and testability of navigation flows.

  1. SwiftUI-based apps:
    • SwiftUI introduces declarative navigation, which naturally reduces the need for an external coordinator.
    • That said, State-driven Coordinators or Composable Architecture (TCA) can replace traditional coordinators in SwiftUI.
  2. Very small apps or prototypes:
    • Adding coordinators may introduce unnecessary complexity.
  3. Cross-platform frameworks (like Flutter or React Native):
    • Other navigation paradigms and state management tools are typically favored.
