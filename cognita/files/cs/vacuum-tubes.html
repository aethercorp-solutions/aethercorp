Vacuum tubes

Vacuum tubes (also called electron tubes or thermionic valves) are electronic devices that control the flow of electric current in a vacuum inside a sealed glass or metal tube. They were widely used in early computers, radios, televisions, and amplifiers before the invention of transistors.

Inside the vacuum tube:
