Line Items

In an inventory management system, line items refer to the individual entries or components within a larger transaction record ‚Äî such as a purchase order, sales order, invoice, or stock transfer.

Think of a line item as one distinct product entry in a transaction.

A line item represents one product (or SKU) along with its specific details ‚Äî like quantity, unit price, discount, and total amount ‚Äî within a transaction document.

In other words:

A line item = one row in the list of items involved in a transaction.

Line items appear in almost every type of inventory transaction:

  • Purchase Orders (POs) ‚Äì items being ordered from suppliers

üß© What Each Line Item Typically Contains

Field Description
Item ID / SKU Unique identifier for the product
Description Name or short details of the product
Quantity How many units are involved
Unit of Measure (UoM) e.g., pcs, box, kg, liter
Unit Price / Cost Price per unit
Discounts / Taxes Any applied discounts or tax details
Total Value Quantity √ó Unit Price ¬± discounts/taxes
Warehouse / Location Where the item is stored or sent
Batch / Lot Number (optional) For tracking expiry or manufacturing batches

üìò Why Line Items Are Important

  • Allow granular tracking of what items were involved in a transaction
  • Enable partial updates (e.g., only one item received out of three ordered)
  • Provide the basis for inventory valuation and cost calculations
  • Make it easy to audit transactions item by item
